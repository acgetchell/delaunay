\begin{abstract}
  The validation architecture in \texttt{delaunay} separates local object
  validity, combinatorial consistency, intrinsic piecewise-linear topology,
  embedding validity, and geometric predicates. This separation is useful both
  mathematically and in software: PL-manifoldness remains an intrinsic property
  of the abstract simplicial complex, while Euclidean, toroidal, spherical, and
  future embedding models specialize only the extrinsic realization and
  geometric predicate layers.
\end{abstract}

\keywords{Delaunay triangulation, validation, piecewise-linear
topology, computational geometry, Rust}

\section{Validation Architecture}

The central design principle is that validation layers answer different
questions. The first three layers are independent of the embedding, while the
last two are embedding-dependent. This decomposition lets the same triangulation
data structure and bistellar move machinery serve Euclidean, toroidal, and
spherical workflows without redefining PL topology for each ambient model.

\subsection{Element Validity}

Element Validity asks whether individual objects are internally well-formed.
For vertices this includes finite coordinates, a non-nil UUID, and the expected
dimension. For simplices this includes exactly \(d+1\) distinct vertex
references, valid local identity, and any owner-local orientation convention.
This layer is deliberately local: it does not decide whether two simplices fit
together, whether the complex is a manifold, or whether the coordinates realize
the complex without overlap.

\subsection{Combinatorial Consistency}

Combinatorial Consistency asks whether the triangulation data structure
represents a coherent abstract simplicial complex. This includes key/UUID
mappings, simplex incidence, reciprocal neighbor references, bounded facet
multiplicity, ridge connectivity, and coherent combinatorial orientation. These
checks still do not depend on a metric or embedding. A complex can pass this
layer and still fail to be a PL manifold or fail to embed faithfully.

\subsection{Intrinsic PL Topology}

Intrinsic PL Topology asks whether the abstract complex has the requested PL
topological type. For a \(k\)-simplex \(\sigma\) in a \(d\)-dimensional
PL-manifold with boundary, the link condition is
\[
  \mathrm{lk}(\sigma) \cong
  \begin{cases}
    S^{d-k-1}, & \sigma \text{ is interior},\\
    B^{d-k-1}, & \sigma \text{ is boundary}.
  \end{cases}
\]
The implementation enforces this through facet-degree checks, boundary closure,
ridge-link and vertex-link certification as configured by
\texttt{TopologyGuarantee}, connected components, isolated-vertex rejection, and
Euler characteristic checks. These invariants are the PL-topology layer of the
library, in the same spirit as the intrinsic bistellar-equivalence theory
underlying Pachner moves~\cite{pachner1991,lickorish_simplicial_1999}.

This layer is intrinsic. It is unchanged by replacing a Euclidean embedding
with a toroidal or spherical one. In particular, spherical support does not
require a new definition of PL-manifoldness; it requires spherical embedding and
spherical Delaunay predicates in the later layers.

\subsection{Embedding Validity}

Embedding Validity asks whether the abstract complex is faithfully realized in
the selected ambient model. In Euclidean affine charts this requires
nondegenerate maximal simplices and no intersections except along shared faces.
Toroidal embeddings apply the same idea in periodic covering-space charts.
Spherical embeddings instead require vertices to lie on
\(S^d \subset \mathbb{R}^{d+1}\) and require spherical cells to be
nondegenerate on that embedded sphere.

This layer is separate from Delaunay-ness. A triangulation can be a valid
embedding without being Delaunay, and a Delaunay predicate is not meaningful on
a folded or self-intersecting embedding.

\subsection{Geometric Predicates}

Geometric Predicates ask whether a valid embedding satisfies a selected
geometry-specific condition. The implemented predicate family today is
Delaunay. In Euclidean and toroidal affine charts this is the empty circumsphere
condition, implemented with robust geometric predicates in the style of
Shewchuk~\cite{shewchuk1997} and classical Delaunay triangulation theory.
Intrinsic Delaunay constructions on Riemannian manifolds provide the broader
geometric setting for this separation~\cite{leibon_delaunay_2000}.
For spherical \(d\)-D Delaunay triangulations, input points are normalized in
\(\mathbb{R}^{d+1}\), the construction uses the ambient convex hull, and
spherical cells correspond to outward hull
facets~\cite{edelsbrunner2001,caroli2010}. The intrinsic dimension
remains \(d\); the extra coordinate belongs to
the spherical embedding backend, not to the abstract simplex dimension.

Future predicate families, such as regular triangulations, weighted Delaunay,
Gabriel graphs, alpha complexes, constrained Delaunay variants, or hyperbolic
predicates, should specialize this layer without changing the first three
layers.

\subsection{Extensibility}

The decomposition can be summarized as
\[
  \text{PL topology}
  \perp
  \text{embedding topology}
  \perp
  \text{geometric predicates}.
\]
This is the useful software boundary. Bistellar moves operate on the abstract
simplicial complex and preserve PL topology when their local preconditions are
met. Embedding backends then decide whether the edited complex is faithfully
realized, and predicate backends decide whether the realized complex is
Delaunay or satisfies another geometric condition.
